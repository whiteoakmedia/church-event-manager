Why it matters

The check-in table just got a lot faster.

For most of our churches, the bottleneck on event mornings isn't capacity — it's the line at the table while a volunteer searches for someone's name on a list. QR check-in collapses that to a single scan.

Seconds, not minutes

One scan = one check-in. A line of 30 people clears in under a minute.

No app to install

Volunteers use the existing check-in screen in any browser. Registrants use their email.

Quiet on the back end

Every scan logs the same audit trail as a manual check-in. Your reports don't change.

For your registrants

What attendees see and do.

No extra step on their end. Their existing confirmation email just got a little smarter — and they don't have to do anything until they walk in the door.

  1. They register normally Same form, same flow as before. Nothing changes here.
  2. Their confirmation email now includes a personal QR A black-and-white square code, plus the words "Show this code at check-in." Same email that already has the date, time, location, and a link to manage their registration.
  3. On the day of the event, they pull the email up on their phone They don't even have to open the email — most phones surface the QR right in the preview. If they lose the email, the same QR also appears on the "Manage My Registration" page.
  4. They walk in. Volunteer scans. Done. No name spelling, no clipboard hunting, no "are you in the system?"
A bonus you didn't ask for: the same email also got "Save to your calendar" buttons for Google, Apple, and Outlook. One tap puts the event on their calendar with the right title, time, and location pre-filled. Fewer no-shows because nobody forgot.
For your team

How to scan people in on Sunday morning.

Anyone who can use the existing check-in screen can use the scanner — there's nothing new to learn beyond pointing a camera at a code.

  1. Open the Check-In screen in your WordPress admin Church Events → Check-In. Use any device with a camera: laptop, iPad, or your phone. The site needs to load on https:// (yours does — required for camera access).
  2. Pick today's event from the dropdown Events happening today are marked "TODAY" so you can't grab the wrong one. The volunteer list and the scan button only become active once an event is selected.
  3. Tap "📷 Scan QR" A modal opens with a live camera preview. First time you do this on a new device, the browser will ask permission to use the camera — say yes once and it remembers.
  4. Hold the camera up to each registrant's QR As soon as the code is in frame, you'll hear a soft "ka-ching" chime and a green "✓ Checked in: [Name]" banner flashes. The scanner stays open — just keep moving to the next person. Phones display the QR in the email preview, so most registrants don't even need to open the email.
  5. The line clears Need to add a walk-in? Hit "Add Walk-in" right next to the scan button. Need to find someone whose phone died? The name search still works exactly as before. Use whichever fits the moment.
Common questions

What we've already been asked.

What if someone shows me the wrong QR code?
The scanner checks the code against the current event's registrants. If it's not in that event, you'll see a red "Not registered for this event" banner with a low buzz — no risk of checking someone into the wrong service.
What if someone scans their QR twice?
The second scan shows an amber "Already checked in: [Name]" banner. No double counts, no confusion.
What if a registrant doesn't have their phone?
The name search on the check-in screen works exactly like it did before — type a few letters, tap their card. The QR is a faster lane, not the only lane.
What if our internet is spotty?
The scanner runs in the browser and checks codes against a list already loaded for the event. You only need internet to load the page at the start of the service.
Does this cost more?
No. It's included with your existing plugin at no extra cost. The QR codes are generated on your own site — nothing is sent to a third-party service.
Can attendees print the QR instead of using their phone?
Yes. The QR is on the manage-registration page as well as in the email — they can print either one, and the scanner reads it off paper just as easily as off a screen.
