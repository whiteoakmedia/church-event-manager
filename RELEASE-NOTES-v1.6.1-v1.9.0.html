v1.9.0 May 12, 2026

Per-event check-in toggle

Not every event needs attendance tracking. A food drive, a fundraiser, a drop-off collection — those don't need QR codes cluttering the confirmation email. New toggle in the event editor decides.

In the event editor

  • New checkbox under Sign-Up Settings: "Enable check-in for this event." Default unchecked. Opt-in per event.

What it gates

  • Confirmation email — QR code block only renders when check-in is on.
  • Manage page — QR card only renders when check-in is on.
  • Check-In screen dropdown — only events with check-in enabled appear there. Volunteers can't accidentally pick the wrong event.
  • Walk-in endpoint — refuses requests for non-check-in events, server-side defense.

One-time cleanup needed

  • After updating, open each event that actually needs check-in (CR Vet Dinner, OneMeal Packing, Park Outreach, Snack & Swap, Church Picnic, etc.) and tick the new box. ~5 minutes total.
v1.8.3 — 1.8.6 May 12, 2026

Multi-day events show a date range on the card

Cards used to only show the start date for events spanning multiple days, which made it impossible to know when something actually ended. Now: a compact pill above the title reads "APR 30 → DEC 1, 2026" so the full range — including year — is visible at a glance.

  • Same-year ranges show one year on the end: MAY 1 → MAY 28, 2026.
  • Cross-year ranges show year on both: DEC 31, 2026 → JAN 5, 2027.
  • Single-day events keep the existing prominent square date badge.
  • Three iterations of design polish to get the spacing right and stop the chip from squashing the rest of the card body on long ranges.
v1.8.4 May 12, 2026

Date-entry guardrail on event save

A year-typo on an event's end date (e.g. 2027 instead of 2026) is invisible until it bites you — the event displays the right start date but never expires from the upcoming list. Two new save-time checks catch this.

  • Hard block: if end is before start, the end value isn't saved and you get a red error notice: "The end date is before the start date — your end date was not saved."
  • Soft warning: if end is more than a year after start, the save proceeds but a yellow banner appears: "Heads up — this event runs for over a year. Did you mean to set the end date that far out?"
  • No interruption for normal saves. Only fires when something's actually off.
v1.8.2 May 12, 2026

Defense against stale events on the listing

A trashed event was still appearing on the public listing because an upstream CDN was serving a cached version of the page. Even though the plugin's query already excluded the event, we added three layers of defensive filtering so a trashed or past event can never paint regardless of how stale the cache is.

  • Query, post-query filter, and render loop each authoritatively re-check post_status and end-date.
  • Reads directly from the WordPress posts table at each stage — can't be fooled by stale post-meta or query filters.
v1.8.1 · hotfix May 10, 2026

Group meeting times no longer shift by hours

Walking Group's Wednesday meeting was showing as 5pm on the public site but 12pm in admin — a 5-hour drift. Root cause: a timezone mismatch when rendering the group's time field. Fixed so admin and public always agree.

v1.8.0 May 10, 2026

QR check-in, webcam scanner, save-to-calendar

The biggest release in this window. Every registrant gets a personal QR code in their confirmation email. Volunteers scan them in with a phone or tablet camera. And every confirmation email got "Save to your calendar" buttons for Google, Apple, and Outlook.

QR codes

  • QR rendered on your own server (no third-party service). Cached as a PNG for fast loads.
  • Appears in the confirmation email and on the registrant's "Manage My Registration" page.
  • Plain black-and-white for maximum scanner reliability on any device.

Webcam check-in scanner

  • New "Scan QR" button on the Check-In screen. Opens a live camera preview.
  • Continuous mode: every successful scan auto-checks the person in. No tap needed. Volunteers walk down the line scanning each phone.
  • Auto-detects rear camera on tablets; switches between cameras if there's more than one.
  • Two-tone chime on success, soft buzz on error. Toggleable.
  • Three states: green "Checked in: [Name]", amber "Already checked in" (no double-counts), red "Not registered for this event."

Save to calendar

  • Three pill buttons in the confirmation email: Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook.
  • One tap puts the event on the registrant's calendar with title, time, location, and event link pre-filled.
  • Covers ~99% of attendees regardless of their email/calendar setup.
v1.7.1 · hotfix May 10, 2026

Registration "Security check failed" fix

Some attendees were getting an error when submitting the registration form. Root cause: a security token baked into the event page was expiring after 24 hours, but the page itself was being cached for longer. The form now fetches a fresh token at submit time, immune to caching.

  • Plugin-side fix is permanent — handles aggressive page caching, browser-tab-left-open-overnight, anything that makes the page older than its token.
  • User-facing error message also rewritten: "Your session expired — please refresh the page and try again" instead of the technical-sounding original.
  • Failures are now also reported to White Oak Media's monitoring dashboard so we see them before clients have to email about them.
v1.7.0 May 9, 2026

Groups can have multiple meeting times

Walking Group meets Tuesday at 6:30pm, Wednesday at noon, and Saturday at 9am. Before this release, that meant three separate "Walking Group" entries. Now: one group entry, three meeting times listed.

  • New repeater table in the group editor: "+ Add another meeting time" for as many rows as needed.
  • Group cards on the listing page stack all meeting times under the calendar icon.
  • Single-group page lists every time in the schedule block.
  • Event calendar plots each meeting time on its respective day — a group meeting Tue/Wed/Sat shows up three times per week.
  • Existing single-meeting groups carry over automatically. Open one and you'll see the existing schedule already in row 1; tap "+ Add another" to add more.
v1.6.2 May 9, 2026

Group editor gets the UX redesign

Groups now match the event editor's clean card-based layout. Six sections — Description, Type & Status, Schedule, Where it meets, Leadership & Contact, Options — each with its own icon and color-coded status pills.

v1.6.1 May 9, 2026

Event editor UX redesign

The "When & Where" and "Sign-Up Settings" meta boxes were a wall of uppercase labels with a confused field order. Rebuilt into clear visual sections.

  • Six sections, each with its own icon: Date & Time, Location, Organizer, Pricing, Status, Recurrence.
  • Side-by-side fields where they pair naturally (start/end time, organizer name/email).
  • Status replaced from a one-option dropdown into a real Active / Postponed / Cancelled segmented pill control.
  • Cost field has a proper currency-prefix input ($ 25.00) instead of a stretched number box.
  • Sentence-case labels at normal weight — no more uppercase letter-spaced shouting.
