Chapter 1

Adding a new event

The event editor was rebuilt into clean, sectioned cards. Each section handles one job — when, where, who, how much, status, and recurrence.

  1. Go to Church Events → Add New Event From your WordPress admin sidebar.
  2. Type a title at the top This is what shows up on cards and emails. Be specific — "Spring Women's Retreat 2026" beats "Retreat."
  3. Fill in Date & Time Pick a start date and time. End is optional — set it for events with a clear stopping point (dinners, retreats). Skip it for casual drop-ins.
  4. Add Location Venue or room name plus full street address. If you've got a Google Maps link, paste it — registrants get a tappable map link in their confirmation. Toggle "online or hybrid" if there's a stream component.
  5. Set Organizer Name and email of whoever is running the event. The email becomes the "Reply-To" on the confirmation so registrants can reach the right person.
  6. Pricing (optional) Leave blank for free events. Enter 0.00 to display "Free" on the card. For tiered pricing (Adult / Student / Child), use the Pricing tiers section in Sign-Up Settings further down.
  7. Status Three pills: Active (green), Postponed (amber), Cancelled (red). Click whichever applies. Defaults to Active.
  8. Recurrence (optional) Toggle on if this event repeats. Choose Daily / Weekly / Monthly, pick which days, optionally set an end date. The plugin generates the future occurrences for you.
  9. Set capacity + deadlines in Sign-Up Settings Maximum attendees, max guests one person can sign up, sign-up deadline, and "Sign-up status" (Open / Closed). Capacity 0 means unlimited.
  10. Click Publish Event goes live on the events page. Registrations can come in immediately.
Chapter 2

Multi-day events

For events that span multiple calendar days — a Friday-to-Sunday retreat, a week-long camp, a multi-day conference — the card automatically shows a clean date-range pill instead of just a start date.

How it looks on the public site

TypeWhat the card shows
Same-day event Big square pill — month over day, e.g. MAY / 17
Multi-day, same year Compact horizontal pill, year on the end: JUN 6 → JUN 8, 2026
Multi-day, different years Year on both: DEC 31, 2026 → JAN 5, 2027

How to set it up

Nothing different from a normal event. Just set a start date AND an end date that falls on a different calendar day. The card display switches automatically.

Heads up Watch the year. If you accidentally set an end date a year out from when you meant — by mistyping 2027 instead of 2026 — the plugin now catches it. You'll see a yellow warning when you click Update: "Heads up — this event runs for over a year." Double-check the year and update again. If it really is a multi-year event (rare, but real), the warning is dismissible.
Chapter 3

Enabling check-in for an event

Some events need attendance tracking (dinners, retreats, kids' programs). Others don't (food drives, fundraisers, drop-off collections). The plugin makes this a per-event choice so the right events get QR codes and the rest stay clean.

  1. Edit the event Church Events → All Events → click the event title.
  2. Scroll to Sign-Up Settings It's the meta box below "When & Where."
  3. Check "Enable check-in for this event" The second checkbox in the section. Reads: "Adds a QR code to confirmation emails and the registrant's manage page, and lists this event on the Check-In screen."
  4. Update From this moment on, every new confirmation email for this event includes a QR code. Existing registrants get the QR too — it's on their "Manage My Registration" page even if their email predates the toggle.
Decision rule Turn check-in on when you want to know who actually walked through the door. Turn it off when registration alone is the goal (someone signed up, someone got a confirmation, done).

Examples

EventCheck-in?
Easter Service registrationOn — you want a head count at the door
Men's RetreatOn — multi-day event, paid, attendance matters
Kids' CampOn — safety + attendance
Food drive collectionOff — people drop cans, you don't track who showed up
Online giving campaignOff — no in-person attendance
Bake sale (drop-in)Off — public foot traffic, no list
Chapter 4

Running check-in at the event

Volunteers point a phone or tablet camera at each registrant's QR. The scanner stays open and chimes for each successful scan. No app to install.

  1. Open the Check-In screen In WordPress admin: Church Events → Check-In. Use any device with a camera — laptop, iPad, your phone.
  2. Pick the event from the dropdown Only events with check-in enabled appear. Events happening today are tagged "TODAY." Pick the right one — once selected, the scanner becomes available.
  3. Tap "📷 Scan QR" A modal opens with a live camera preview. First time you do this on a device, the browser asks permission to use the camera. Say yes once; it remembers.
  4. Hold the camera up to each registrant's QR As soon as the code's in frame, you'll hear a soft "ka-ching" chime and a green banner reads "✓ Checked in: [Name]." The scanner stays open — move to the next person without tapping anything.
  5. If something's off, the scanner tells you Amber banner: "Already checked in: [Name]" — no double-counts. Red banner with a low buzz: "Not registered for this event" — wrong event or wrong QR. Just keep going.
  6. Close the scanner when the line clears Tap the X or press Escape. The screen returns to the full list, where you can still search by name and tap individual cards for the rest of the event.
Tip Phones typically display the QR right in the email preview — registrants don't even have to open the email. If their phone is dead, the QR is also on their "Manage My Registration" page (link in the confirmation), or use name search instead.
Chapter 5

Adding walk-ins

Someone shows up who didn't register. Instead of bouncing them to the registration form, your volunteer adds them on the spot — registered AND checked in in one tap.

  1. On the Check-In screen, click "+ Add Walk-in" Next to the "Scan QR" button. Only available once an event is selected, and only for events with check-in enabled.
  2. Type first name and last name These are required. Email and phone are optional — leave blank if you don't have them. A placeholder email is generated automatically so the duplicate check still works.
  3. Set number of attendees Defaults to 1. If it's a family of four, enter 4.
  4. Click "Add & Check In" The person is registered, confirmed, AND checked in — all in one shot. The list refreshes automatically so you can see them in the registrant list with a green "Checked In" badge.
Chapter 6

Groups with multiple meeting times

Some groups meet on more than one day — a walking group on Tuesday, Wednesday, AND Saturday. Instead of three separate "Walking Group" entries, you create one group and list all the times under it.

  1. Go to Church Events → Add New Group Or edit an existing group.
  2. Fill in the basics Description, Type & Status, leadership, location — same as before.
  3. Scroll to the Meeting Times section You'll see a repeater table with columns for How Often, Day, Time, and a remove button.
  4. Fill row 1 with your first meeting time e.g. Weekly · Tuesday · 6:30 PM.
  5. Click "+ Add another meeting time" for each additional day Add as many rows as you need. e.g. Weekly · Wednesday · 12:00 PM. Weekly · Saturday · 9:00 AM.
  6. Remove a row by clicking the trash icon on its right The last remaining row can't be deleted — it just clears its values instead.
  7. Click Publish or Update The group's card and detail page will list all the meeting times stacked under the calendar icon. The events calendar (if you use the calendar shortcode) plots each meeting on its respective day.
Migrating If you've already got three "Walking Group" entries because the old version only allowed one meeting time, consolidate: pick one to keep, add the other times to it, then move the duplicates to Trash.
Chapter 7

Bonus features you don't have to do anything for

These work automatically — no setup, no toggles. Just know they're there so you can answer questions when registrants ask.

Save to Calendar buttons

Every confirmation email now has three buttons under the event info: Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook. One tap puts the event on the registrant's calendar with the title, time, location, and link pre-filled. No action needed from you.

Date-typo warnings

When you save an event with weird dates, the plugin catches the obvious mistakes:

  • End before start — saves blocked, red error: "Your end date was not saved." Fix and resubmit.
  • End more than a year out — yellow warning: "Heads up — this event runs for over a year. Did you mean to set the end date that far out?"

"Past" status badge

In the admin events list, every event has a small status pill: Upcoming, Past, Cancelled, or Postponed. Past events stay published (so registrations are preserved for reporting) but disappear from the public listing automatically.

Frequently asked

I changed an event but the website still shows the old version.
Caching. Wait a few minutes or hard-refresh (Cmd+Shift+R on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows). If it's still off after an hour, message us.

A registrant says they got a "Security check failed" error.
This was fixed in v1.7.1 — should no longer happen. If you still see it reported, let us know and we'll investigate.

How do I see who's registered for an event?
Church Events → Registrations. Filter by event in the dropdown. Export CSV if you need it offline.

How do I delete a registration?
From the Registrations table, click the trash icon on the row. You'll get a confirmation prompt that names the person being deleted so you don't fat-finger it.

Why doesn't this event appear on the Check-In screen?
Check-in isn't enabled for it. Edit the event, scroll to Sign-Up Settings, check "Enable check-in for this event," and Update. See Chapter 3.

Can someone print their QR instead of using their phone?
Yes. The QR is on the manage-registration page as well as in the email. Prints fine; the scanner reads it off paper.

What if my internet goes out during check-in?
Once the check-in screen has loaded the event list, scans work against that loaded list. You only need internet when you first open the page.
